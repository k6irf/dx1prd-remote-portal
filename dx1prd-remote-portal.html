Operating Policies
★
Valid amateur radio license required. License class and country of issuance must be provided and verifiable. No unlicensed operation permitted under any circumstance.
★
Club membership or affiliation required. Open to DX1PRD members and invited friends of the club. Applications are reviewed and approved by the station trustee.
✓
Contest, DX chasing, and casual operation are all welcome. Please indicate your intended use so we can allocate time slots fairly across applicants.
✓
Emergency and PAREDN net operations are prioritized. PAREDN-affiliated stations may request priority scheduling for net and activation use.
✕
No unattended operation. You must be actively present at your keyboard whenever the station is transmitting. Timeout timer enforced by the system.
✕
No FT8 or automated digital modes without prior coordination. Contact the sysop before scheduling any digital operation session.
How to Identify on Air
⚠ FCC Requirement — Remote Operators Must Use the Station Callsign
Per FCC 47 CFR §97.119, every amateur station must transmit its assigned callsign at the end of each contact and at least every 10 minutes during a contact. When operating remotely, the station callsign — not your home callsign — is the legally required identification, since it identifies the transmitting station.

"Station ID: DX1PRD, remote op K6DAN"

❌ Incorrect — Do NOT do this on either station
"This is DV1K"
Using only your home callsign does not legally identify the transmitting station. Always give the station callsign (DX1PRD or K6UD) first.

Situation Required

Language for ID English required (FCC §97.119)
CW identification DX1PRD DE (your call) or K6UD DE (your call)
Why mention you are a remote operator? While not strictly required by the FCC, it is good amateur practice and courtesy to mention you are operating remotely. It helps the other station understand why your callsign district (6) may not match your location, avoids confusion, and promotes transparency in the amateur community. PAREDN encourages all remote operators to identify as shown above.
🏆 Contest Operation Exception

During contest operations where club members are participating as a club entry, there is no requirement to announce the remote operator callsign. The FCC has no specific rule mandating individual operator identification during contest exchanges. Simply use the station callsign alone:

📡 DX1PRD Station:
"59 DX1PRD"
"This is DX1PRD"
📡 KE6YEP Station:
"59 K6UD"
"This is K6UD"

You must still transmit the station callsign at least every 10 minutes and at the end of each contact as required by FCC §97.119. The contest exchange callsign counts as your legal station ID. Adding your personal callsign during contests is optional but always welcome.
